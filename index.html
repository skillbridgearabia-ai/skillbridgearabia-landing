The Problem

A workforce that arrives unprepared, every single year.

Six in ten expat workers who join Saudi Arabia’s food and beverage sector have no formal training. There is no scalable way to fix that before they land. Until now.

🏢
For Employers
Weeks of onboarding that should not be necessary
  • Two to four weeks training every new hire from scratch
  • Language barriers making service inconsistent daily
  • Four in ten workers leave within the first 90 days
  • No way to verify skills before someone starts
  • The same problems repeating with every new intake
👨
For Workers
Starting a new life with nothing to show for it
  • Arriving with no certified skills on paper
  • No Arabic, no English, no way to communicate well
  • Uncertified workers earn 50 to 100% less than certified ones in the same role
  • No clear path to better positions or higher pay
  • Vulnerable from day one, often without proper documentation
📋
For Agencies
Failed placements that damage everyone’s reputation
  • A worker without their MHRSD food safety certificate cannot legally work — the placement fails entirely
  • Nothing to separate a quality candidate from the rest
  • Client complaints that are hard to address without a systemic fix
  • Revenue lost every time a placement falls through
  • Saudization pressure making skilled, compliant expat candidates more valuable than ever

How It Works

Workers learn on their phones before they land. Employers track every step.

SkillBridge Arabia is a mobile training platform built entirely around the realities of this industry. Short lessons, practical skills, bilingual content and certificates that employers actually recognise.

🏗
Certified Skills Courses
Barista, front of house, kitchen helper and retail staff programmes, each built around SCA standards and the Saudi hospitality market.
🗣️
Arabic for the Workplace
The phrases workers actually need: taking orders, handling complaints, greeting customers, understanding their rights. Practical from lesson one.
📱
Fully Mobile, Four Languages
Workers start learning in Bangladesh, India or Nepal before they ever board a flight. iOS, Android and web, in English, Arabic, Bengali and Hindi.
📉
Employer Dashboard
See exactly where each worker is in their training, which lessons they have completed and which certificates they have earned. All in one place.
🏆
Verifiable Certificates
Each certificate carries a QR code so any employer can verify it instantly. Includes MHRSD food safety compliance, aligned with HRDF and Saudi Vision 2030.
🤖
AI Arabic Coach
Workers can practise Arabic conversation at any time of day. The coach gives instant feedback and adapts to their specific role and level.
🌿
Sustainable Service
Every programme includes a module on waste reduction, plastic regulations and green kitchen practice. Built for where Saudi F&B regulations are heading, not just where they are today.

Why Now
☕
The Saudi food service sector is worth $25.99 billion and accelerating
Over 3,500 new coffee shops registered in KSA in 2024 alone. The QSR segment is growing fastest. Every single one of those businesses needs trained staff and almost none of them have a reliable way to get them.
👨‍🏫
120,000 new F&B workers enter Saudi Arabia every year, most of them unprepared
They arrive without food safety certificates, without Arabic, without any proof of skill. MHRSD and Balady regulations require those certificates. The gap between what workers have and what the law demands is our market.
🇨🇦
Nobody has built this for this market before
We have done the research. There is no digital platform targeting pre-arrival training for GCC frontline expat workers. That will not be true for long, which is why moving now matters.
📈
Saudization pressure is making trained expats more valuable, not less
As Nitaqat quotas tighten for management roles, frontline positions remain expat-reliant. Agencies that can send certified, compliant candidates command better fees and lose fewer placements.
